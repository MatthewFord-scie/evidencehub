About the Evidence Hub

The Social Care Equity Evidence Hub is a central resource for evidence and analysis on equity in social care. It gathers research, case studies, and data from a range of sources to provide a clear picture of how social care services are experienced by different groups. The hub offers tools, reports, and insights that help professionals and decision-makers develop policies and practices that promote fairness. By sharing reliable evidence, the hub aims to drive change towards a social care system that treats all individuals equitably.

What do we mean by equity?

Equity in social care means ensuring that everyone has fair access to the services and support they need, based on their individual circumstances. It involves recognising that people have different needs and may face various barriers, so resources and care are distributed in a way that helps everyone achieve similar health and wellbeing outcomes.


Included evidence

In order to be included on the Evidence Hub, all content must:

  • Relate specifically to care equity and access issues within the social care system.
  • Be grounded in robust research that highlights disparities in access and care equity.
  • Provide a comprehensive description of the research methods used, detailing the study's location, timing, and researchers involved.
  • Outline the context in which the research was conducted, including any relevant cultural, social, or political factors that may have shaped the findings.
  • Detail the research participants, including demographics and the methodology for selecting the sample.
  • Evidence all conclusions drawn and provide justification for any recommendations made.
  • Acknowledge any uncertainty or limitations inherent in the research findings.

Please refer to our detailed set of inclusion criteria and methods of appraising the strength of the evidence for further guidance.
