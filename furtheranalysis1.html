Further Analysis

The chart below visually represents the percentages of adults, by ethnicity, in England receiving long-term support from social care services, alongside the overall ethnic percentages within the population.
This dual representation provides insight into social care equity, highlighting potential disparities across different ethnic groups.



The chart indicates significant variations in the percentage of adults receiving long-term support among ethnic groups. For instance, the data shows that while 79.6% of long-term support recipients identify as White British, the overall percentage of this group within the population is lower. This disparity suggests that White British individuals may be accessing social care services at a disproportionately higher rate compared to other ethnic groups.

Groups such as Indian, Pakistani, and Black African individuals show relatively low percentages of long-term support recipients, despite representing a sizeable segment of the population. This raises concerns about accessibility and equity in social care provision for these communities.
